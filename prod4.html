Upcycled DIY Project: Custom Printed T-Shirt

Old T-Shirt

Fabric Paint or Acrylic

Cling film

Iron

Masking Tape

How to Make It

Sketch Your Design: Use markers, colored pencils, or crayons to draw your custom artwork on plain white paper. Keep the shapes bold and colorful for best results.

Cut It Out: Carefully cut around your drawing, leaving a small edge around the design.

Wrap It with Plastic: Cover the entire front of your cut-out design with plastic wrap (cling film), folding any extra wrap behind the paper.

Position the Design: Lay your t-shirt flat and place the wrapped design exactly where you want it printed. Plastic side should face the fabric.

Cover with Baking Paper: Place a sheet of baking or parchment paper over the design to protect it while ironing.

Iron It On: With the iron set to medium-high (no steam), press down on the design for 1–2 minutes. Move the iron gently in circles to evenly apply heat.

Let It Cool: Allow the design to cool completely, then carefully peel off the baking paper. Your art should now be transferred!

Ready to Wear: You’ve got a totally custom, upcycled tee! Style it, gift it, or make a whole collection.
