How to Make Tin Planters

Materials Needed

  • Empty tin can (clean and label-free)
  • Hammer and nail or drill (for drainage holes)
  • Potting soil
  • Small plant or seeds
  • Spoon or small trowel
  • Water

Optional for decoration: acrylic or spray paint, twine, washi tape, stickers, waterproof sealer

Instructions

Step 1: Clean the Tin Can

Wash the can thoroughly, remove the label, and make sure there are no sharp edges. You can file the rim or cover it with tape for safety.

Step 2: Create Drainage Holes

Turn the can upside down and carefully make 3â€“4 small holes in the bottom using a hammer and nail or a drill. This helps prevent water buildup.

Step 3: Decorate the Can (Optional)

If you want to personalize your planter, paint or decorate it before adding soil. Let the paint dry completely and seal it if you plan to use the planter outside.

Step 4: Add Soil and Plant

Fill the can about 3/4 full with potting soil. Place your plant or seeds in the center and gently cover with more soil.

Step 5: Water and Care

Water your plant lightly and place the can in a spot that gets appropriate sunlight. Keep an eye on the soil moisture and enjoy watching your plant grow!
