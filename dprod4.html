Jeans Pocket Wall Organizer

Materials Needed

  • Old jeans (with back pockets)
  • Scissors
  • Hot glue gun or fabric glue
  • Cardboard, canvas, or thick fabric (for the backing)
  • Needle and thread or hole punch and string (for hanging)
  • Optional: Paint, ribbon, stickers, patches, or other decorations
  • Wall hook or pin

Instructions

Step 1: Cut Out the Pockets

Carefully cut the back pockets from the jeans, keeping a small border of fabric around the edges to make gluing easier.

Step 2: Prepare the Backing

Cut a large rectangle out of cardboard, canvas, or thick fabric. This will serve as the base for your organizer.

Step 3: Arrange the Pockets

Lay the pockets onto the backing in your preferred arrangement. Make sure there is space between them to avoid overlap.

Step 4: Attach the Pockets

Use hot glue or fabric glue to firmly attach each pocket to the backing. Alternatively, sew the pockets for added durability.

Step 5: Decorate

If desired, decorate your organizer using paint, ribbons, patches, or other craft materials to give it a personalized look.

Step 6: Add a Hanging Method

Punch holes at the top and add string or thread to create a loop for hanging. You may also sew a loop directly onto the backing.

Step 7: Display Your Organizer

Hang your finished wall organizer on a hook or nail. Use the pockets to store small items like stationery, notes, or tools.
